# Party Mode Workflow

A specialized brainstorming environment for resolving complex architectural cross-domain conflicts (e.g., when the UI/UX need contradicts the DBA's optimization strategy).

## Phase 1: The Arena

The Scrum Master establishes the exact conflict holding up development.

- "We are debating X vs Y. The stakeholders involve BE, FE, and QA."

## Phase 2: The Tribunal

1. **Agent 1 (e.g., Architect)**: Delivers their uncompromising technical stance on the problem.
2. **Agent 2 (e.g., Frontend)**: Counters with user experience impacts and cognitive load constraints.
3. **Agent 3 (e.g., QA)**: Points out edge cases or testability issues with both approaches.

## Phase 3: The Verdict

The PM (Product Manager) reviews the technical inputs and makes an executive decision prioritizing DevForge's core principles (Security, Readability, Scalability). The verdict is logged, the `core-memory.md` is updated, and Dev execution begins immediately. No further debate is allowed after the Verdict.
